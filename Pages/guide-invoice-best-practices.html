Invoice Best Practices for Small Businesses

Use this guide to create invoices that are easy to approve, easy to pay and easy to record.

Short Summary

The best invoices are clear, itemized, branded, legally careful and simple to pay.

Best Practices

  • Use a unique invoice number for every document.
  • Show business and client details clearly.
  • Break work into understandable line items.
  • State taxes and discounts separately.
  • Use payment terms that match your cash-flow needs.
  • Add bank details, payment links or reference numbers.
  • Save receipts after payment is received.

Common Mistakes

Avoid vague descriptions, missing due dates, incorrect tax rates, hidden payment instructions and inconsistent invoice numbers.

Follow-Up Workflow

Send the invoice immediately, remind the client before the due date, follow up on the due date, then send a polite overdue notice with the invoice number and payment link.

FAQ

When should I send an invoice? Send it when work is completed, at a milestone, or before work begins if a deposit is required.

Should I use quotes before invoices? Yes, quotes help clients approve scope and price before you bill.
